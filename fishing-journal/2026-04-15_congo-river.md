## What Worked (User-Confirmed)
- **Quadpod at Local School Boat Dock** → 50% of the Congo expedition panfish/moderate-cat completion in one sitting
- **Cable Ferry shore** → remaining panfish completion + some heavier cats reachable
- **Boat across Cable Ferry, mid-river drop** → two heaviest bottom rods (Mokonzi + Tribal Totem) + Nile Chasseur active = trophy engine
- **Nile Chasseur 6'3" SE + GiantSpin 9000 SE + Craw 6" on 1 1/2 Oz #8/0 jighead** = big-cat MVP. Drop-and-lift technique: drop to bottom, reel 3 sec, lift rod tip, pause, repeat. Hits on the pause.
- **Large Cutbait + Cap'n Hook #7/0** on Tribal Totem = 189 lb Trophy Kamba engine
- **African Giant Water Bug** = trophy-tier Kamba producer
- **Canned Meat + Cap'n Hook #10/0** on Humenus = mid-class Kamba (96.9 lb)
- **Corn on Brutus float** = panfish / labeo / bulldog volume

## What Didn't Work
- **Heavy Chaser 9'10" SE + RedFish 5500 (57 lb class)** — BROKE THE REEL on 2nd cast
  - Congo big cats overwhelmed the 57 lb drag. Left location, repaired, and left this rod off the Congo loadout permanently.
  - **Lesson:** Sub-60 lb tackle has no place on Congo. Minimum reel class here is ~70-80 lb (Ossibus Blue, Savana Spirit, L'impulsion tier).
- **Silverthorn Stake 7'6" + Vampire's Bane 7500 (88 lb)** + JigHead 1 1/2 Oz #8/0 + Craw 6"
  - Hooked a huge cat but **could not gain ground — fish held at 150'+ out** for an extended fight
  - Eventually let line go slack to slip the hook (no equipment damage)
  - **Lesson:** 88 lb line is the threshold — trophy Kamba live past it. Jump to Nile Chasseur (97 lb) for any mid-river active rod duty.
  - **Fix deployed in-session:** switched to Nile Chasseur, caught 2 big cats on the very next sequence of casts.
