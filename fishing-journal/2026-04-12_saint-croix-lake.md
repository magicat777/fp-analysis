## Spot Change: Floating Dock (replacing Fairytale Docks)
Major tactical shift this session:

**Why Floating Dock is superior:**
- **360° lure fishing** — can cast spinning/casting rods in every direction (Fairytale was limited to one arc)
- **Shorter bottom rod range** — key catfish/sturgeon spots are 75-125 ft off the dock vs 150-250 ft at Fairytale
- **4-Rod Stand faces shore** from middle of dock, covering the entire catfish/sturgeon strip without needing long bombs
- **No dual-hookup timeouts** — closer range means faster retrieval, so two simultaneous cats no longer risk timeout on one while reeling the other
- **Buoy markers from completed missions** mark exact fish locations — can target specific species zones precisely

**Drawback:**
- **Higher repair bills** — the heavy usage at closer range seems to wear gear faster (14,059 for one repair cycle)

## Casting Rod Testing Results
Tested multiple casting options for long-range 3-25 lb fish (trout, bass, walleye):

| Rod Tested | Result |
|------------|--------|
| **GalaxyCast 8'6" + VorteX 7000 SE** | **WINNER** — longest casts, best reach for marked spots |
| Various others | Tried multiple setups, each return trip cost income |
| Salmonster 8'10" (spinning comparison) | Hit just as much, but with less sensitivity — same drop-and-raise effectiveness |

**GalaxyCast 8'6" Findings:**
- X-Heavy casting, 7/8-2 1/8 Oz lure, 14.6-83.8 Lb line
- Paired with VorteX 7000 SE reel for maximum spool/range
- **Braid .0076"** for ultra-long casts (thinnest line = least air drag)
- Great for 3-10 lb fish with good sensitivity on the drop
- **Lure weight calibration is tricky** — finding the right weight for correct drop speed was challenging
- **Technique confirmed:** slow drop + fast raise = consistent hits (same as Salmonster/Zeus findings)
- The Salmonster hits just as many fish but with less sensitivity — likely the Kraken 8000 SE's heavier feel smooths out the feedback
